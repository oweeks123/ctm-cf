Minor Injuries Unit Criteria

Inclusion:
  • Minor injuries with definite mechanism of injury
  • Limb injuries
  • Animal bites
  • Human bites
  • Needle-stick injuries
  • Minor traumatic wounds
  • Nail injuries
  • Paronychia
  • Lacerations
  • Burns – not excessive burns
  • Minor head and facial injuries over 1 years of age – no to loc/vomiting
  • Non orthopaedic plaster problems
    •  Band 7 ENP only
  • Back injuries
  • Chest injuries
  • Eye injuries
  • Minor self harm
    •
    Exclusion:
  • Non injury complaints
  • Significant trauma > 6 feet fall
  • AbdoAll other wounds/bitesminal injury
  • Hip/pelvic injury
  • Non traumatic calf pain
  • Epistaxis
    •
    Animal / Insect Bites
    ED
  • Severe pain
  • Any facial/tongue swelling
  • Any difficulty breathing
  • Complete numbness of area below wound
  • Difficulty controlling bleeding
    •  MIU
  • All other wounds/bites
  • Any minor reaction to bite/sting
  • Minor infected bite wounds
  • Any swelling itching around bite/sting
    •
    Limb Injury
    ED
  • Any wounds to skin over suspected fracture(excluding graze)
  • Extremity white/blue
  • Severe pain
  • Bone visible
  • Hip injury
  • Any suspicion from caller of NAI
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Obvious deformity
    •  MIU
  • Unable to weight bear
  • Swelling
  • Moderate pain
  • All other recent limb injury
    •
    Facial Injury
    ED
  • Any suspicion of NAI
  • Babies under 1 year
  • Any loss of consciousness
  • More than 2 episodes of vomiting
  • Active epistaxis
  • Severe pain
    •  MIU
  • All other facial injuries / wounds
    •
    Foreign Bodies
    ED
  • Inhaled / ingested or swallowed foreign bodies
  • Foreign bodies in rectum
  • Foreign body in vagina
    •  MIU
  • Foreign body in ear, nose
    •
    Burns
    ED
  • Electrical burn (i.e. electric shock)
  • Burn from house fire
  • Any history of smoke inhalation
  • Burn to neck/face/head
  • Severe pain
  • Extensive burn
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Burn completely circling extremity
  • White painless burned area
  • Any suspicion from caller of non accidental injury
    •  MIU
  • All other recent burns
    •
    Neck Injury
    ED
  • Direct trauma to neck i.e hit with a blunt object
  • Any numbness/weakness to limbs
  • Dangerous mechanism of injury i.e :-
    1. Fall greater than 1 metre/fall down more than 5 steps
    2. Axial load to head i.e fall onto head/diving accident
    3. High speed ( over 30mph ) RTC/car rolled over /extricated from car /ejected from vehicle
    4. Motorcycle/bicycle involved in RTC/ hit from bike by another vehicle
    5. Any loss of consciousness
    6. Any suspicion of non accidental injury
    7. Severe pain/ vastly reduced range of movement of neck
     MIU
  • Patient alert and responding verbally
  • Any low speed rear end shunt rtc (less than 30 mph)
  • Delayed onset of neck pain
  • No mid line c/spine tenderness
  • No neurovascular deficit
  • Able to rotate neck in both directions 45 degrees
    •
    IMMEDIATE ED
  • Airway compromise
  • Unstable respiratory problems
  • Severe wounds
  • Deep penetrating wounds
  • Severe trauma
  • Severe crush injuries
  • Limb injuries with absence of pulse or loss of sensation
  • Pain uncontrolled by analgesia
  • Pelvic / hip fractures
  • RTC > 30mph, rollover, ejection, bicycle collision
  • Fall from elevation > 3 feet / 5 stairs, axial load to head
